Enterprise scale dominates the ICP tier. The majority of top-20% accounts are Large Enterprise (2,500+ headcount) or Enterprise (501–2,500), with revenue concentrated at $1B+. No single variable differentiates best customers more consistently than organizational size.
Industrial Engineering is the standout vertical in Round 1. Boeing ($325K lifetime), Clarios ($61K), Magna ($44.4K), and DPR Construction ($44.3K) all land in the top tier. This vertical has high lifetime value and long tenure — a defining characteristic of Round 1's ICP that was not yet fully reflected in later acquisition activity.
CS+SaaS drives top-tier performance. 20 of 27 top accounts run the hybrid model. Repeat deal behavior and tenure compound over time, and the CS+SaaS structure creates the conditions for both. All three perfect-score accounts (Gilead, Dell, Thomas Jefferson University) are CS+SaaS with 4+ year tenures.
Cinebody Cold Email and Referral are the dominant ICP channels. Cold email accounts for roughly half of top-tier lead origins; Referral is second. Note: Cold email was retired in April 2025 due to declining efficacy. Its dominance here is a historical signal, not a replicable channel going forward.
Bristol-Myers Squibb ($372K) and UCHealth ($366K) dropped to 2nd quintile due to churn. Both are among the highest-lifetime-value accounts in the entire dataset. Their churn is not an ICP signal — these are lost enterprise relationships worth investigating separately for recovery potential.

The Anti-ICP archetype is consistent and clear. CS Only product, single deal, sub-12-month lifespan, small company (Micro or Small band), sub-$10M revenue, Storygen or no known origin. This pattern holds across nearly all bottom-tier accounts and reflects a targeting problem — specifically, the parameters driving Storygen outreach at the time this cohort was acquired.
The DOOR appears in the Round 1 Anti-ICP tier. At time of this analysis it was a brand-new CS Only account with a single deal and no expansion. By Q1 2026, The DOOR had become an active account with ongoing campaigns and material expansion potential. This is a concrete example of why active CS Only accounts should not be treated as Anti-ICP — the model scores realized behavior, not trajectory.

Priority Actions — From Round 1

01
Tighten Storygen targeting toward enterprise firmographics. Anti-ICP is overwhelmingly Storygen-sourced, sub-$10M, Micro/Small companies. Brief should target 250+ headcount, $50M+ revenue. Storygen is the primary active channel — output quality is a targeting problem, not a vendor problem.
02
Prioritize Industrial Engineering outreach proactively. Boeing, Magna, Clarios, and DPR are among the highest-value accounts in the dataset. Recent starts show only one Industrial account in the top tier. This vertical requires active outbound — it is not coming through inbound or Storygen at current parameters.
03
Investigate Bristol-Myers Squibb and UCHealth churn. Combined $738K+ lifetime value. Both are long-tenure, high-spend accounts that churned. Understanding exit reasons and assessing recovery potential is a higher-value activity than most prospecting at this stage.
04
Develop a referral growth strategy. Referral accounts for 26% of the top-20% and produces consistently high-quality customers. With cold email retired, referral is the highest-quality remaining channel. Systematizing referral generation from existing top-tier accounts (Gilead, Dell, Jefferson, Macerich, Boeing) should be a near-term priority.
Health Score Methodology — Round 1 (Q4 2025)
Lookback WindowJul 1, 2024 – Dec 31, 2025 (18 months). Inclusion criterion: Max Invoice Date within window.
Scoring Model7-dimension point system, max score 60. Quartile thresholds calculated from this cohort.
Points: StatusActive = 10, Churned = 0
Points: Product ClassCS+SaaS = 10, SaaS = 8, CS Only = 3, Free = 0
Points: Spend LevelAvg. Spend/Year quartile-based — 10/7/4/2
Points: Repeat BuyerDeals Won — 5+ = 10 / 3–4 = 7 / 2 = 4 / 1 = 1 (threshold-based)
Points: Company RevenueAnnual Revenue quartile-based — 10/7/4/2, then multiplied by 0.5 weight. Max contribution: 5 pts.
Points: Company HeadcountHead Count quartile-based — 10/7/4/2, then multiplied by 0.5 weight. Max contribution: 5 pts. Revenue and Headcount were weighted at 0.5 each because of high correlation between the two variables — effectively combining them into a single 10-point size dimension. This is the primary reason max score is 60 in Round 1 vs. 70 in Round 2, where both dimensions are scored at full weight independently.
Points: TenureEst. Lifespan (Years) quartile-based — 10/7/4/2. Note: Lifespan floored at 1.0 for CS Only accounts in this round. Corrected to actual lifespan in Round 2.
Tier AssignmentTop 20% (n=27, scores 51–60) = ICP. Bottom 20% (n=27, scores 12–27) = Anti-ICP.
Recent Starts CohortMin Invoice Date ≥ Jul 1, 2024. n=63. Scored with fresh quartiles calculated from this subset only.
Round 1 vs Round 2 ChangesThree methodology changes were made in Round 2: (1) Company Revenue and Headcount moved from 0.5 weight each to full independent scoring — primary driver of max score increasing from 60 to 70. (2) SaaS product class scored at 8 pts in R1, refined to 7 pts in R2. (3) CS Only tenure floored at 1.0 year in R1; corrected to actual lifespan in R2. Scores across rounds are directionally comparable but not directly interchangeable due to these changes.
