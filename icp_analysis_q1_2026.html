Size is the dominant signal. 22 of 27 top-tier accounts are Large Enterprise (2,500+ headcount) or Enterprise (501–2,500). Revenue follows the same pattern — the majority sit at $250M+. No other variable differentiates best customers as consistently as organizational scale.
Healthcare and Industrial Engineering are your two validated verticals. Hospital & Health Care (Gilead, MacMillan, Crothall, AdventHealth, Bristol-Myers Squibb, UCHealth) and Mechanical / Industrial Engineering (Clarios, DPR, Magna, Boeing) together account for over a third of top-tier accounts — fully consistent with stated ICP targets.
CS+SaaS is the dominant product class. 20 of 27 top accounts run the hybrid model. The product structure creates stickier, multi-touchpoint relationships — tenure and repeat buyer scores reflect this compounding effect over time.
Bristol-Myers Squibb ($372K) and UCHealth ($366K) are churned top-tier accounts. Both scored 60 due to long tenure and high lifetime spend. These are not Anti-ICP accounts — they are lost enterprise relationships. Investigating exit reasons is more valuable than almost any prospecting activity at this stage.

The Anti-ICP is a single, consistent archetype. CS Only product, one deal, sub-6-month lifespan, Micro or Small company, sub-$10M revenue, sourced via Storygen. This isn't a diverse set of poor fits — it's one repeatable pattern driven by targeting parameters, not channel failure.
Important caveat: active CS Only campaigns are not yet scoreable. Several accounts in the lower tiers have active campaigns where expansion conversations have not yet occurred. The health score model penalizes short tenure and single-deal counts — both of which are structural for any recent CS Only engagement regardless of trajectory. Accounts like Contend ($188K lifetime spend, 5 deals, Agency) and The DOOR scored in the 3rd quintile solely due to recency. The Agency / CS Only / $10M+ subset represents meaningful expansion potential that will be evaluable in the Q2 or Q3 round once conversations conclude.

Cold email dominance is a historical artifact. Cinebody Cold Email was retired in April 2025 due to declining efficacy and volume. Tightening the brief toward 250+ headcount and $50M+ revenue in Healthcare and Industrial verticals is the highest-leverage action this analysis supports.

Priority Actions

01
Tighten Storygen targeting parameters. Current output skews heavily toward sub-$10M, consumer-facing companies. Brief should target 250+ headcount, $50M+ revenue, with Healthcare and Industrial Engineering as priority verticals. Storygen can produce quality accounts when company size is right — this is a brief problem, not a vendor problem.
02
Build intentional Industrial Engineering pipeline. Historical wins (Boeing, Magna, Clarios, DPR) are among the highest-value accounts in the cohort. Recent starts show thin representation in this vertical. Proactive outreach strategy needed — passive inbound is not filling the gap.
03
Investigate Bristol-Myers Squibb and UCHealth churn. Combined lifetime value exceeds $738K. Both were long-tenure, high-spend, top-scoring accounts. Understanding exit reasons is more valuable than almost any prospecting activity at this stage. Recovery potential should be assessed.
04
Revisit Agency / CS Only / $10M+ cohort in Q2 or Q3. Accounts like Contend ($188K lifetime spend) and The DOOR scored poorly due to structural model limitations — short tenure and single-deal counts that are inherent to recent CS Only engagements, not indicators of poor fit. Once expansion conversations conclude, this segment may represent a meaningful ICP addition. Flag active CS Only accounts as "Expansion Pending" in future rounds to distinguish from true one-and-done transactions.
